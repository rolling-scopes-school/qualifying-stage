# Self introduction

The objective of this task is to prepare a concise self-presentation lasting **3–5 minutes**. This exercise is designed 
to help you get ready for future interviews, where a strong self-introduction is essential. Your self-presentation 
shapes the first impression you make, so being well-prepared can significantly impact your success.

In your self-introduction, share information about yourself, explain what sparked your interest in programming, and 
describe your motivation for joining RS School. You can also mention your goals and what you hope to achieve through 
this program. For inspiration, refer to the task you completed: [CV. HTML, CSS & Git Basics](<tasks/cv/html-css-git.md>).

**Important note about English proficiency**:
Most companies and projects now require at least a **B2 level of English**. Our coordinators may point out if your 
English needs improvement. Don’t worry - before the final interview stage, you’ll have the opportunity to enhance your 
English skills.

> ## What to tell about
>
> - Tell us about who you are. Name, primary skill and role. For example, "My name is ... and I'm a junior frontend developer".
> - Tell us briefly about your education. For example, "I am a recent computer science graduate from Belarusian State 
University of Informatics and Radioelectronics and I am currently attending the RSSchool frontend course".
> - Tell us about what you are interested in studying, about your professional plans and about your motivation to do programming.
> - Tell us about projects you've been working on during the course. Describe its purpose, core technologies, challenges encountered, and results.

However, there are no restrictions - you decide what information about yourself to tell.

The presentation must be **in English**. 


## Task steps
1. Once the coordinators share the available time slots, choose the **one** that suits you best and provide your **name** and 
**email** address. The coordinator will send you an invitation link for the **MS Teams** call via **email**. **Please note**: 
by registering for the self-introduction session, you consent to being recorded during the call. The recording will be 
used for internal purposes only and will not be shared outside the RS School team.
2. Ensure your **camera** and **microphone** are **working properly** before the session.
3. Join the call on time and be prepared to introduce yourself in **English** for **3–5** minutes. After your presentation, you 
will answer a few questions related to your introduction in English (no technical questions will be asked). The 
coordinator will assess your presentation and provide feedback on your English proficiency and overall presentation skills.
4. _Don’t panic or worry_, our goal is to help you become well-prepared for future interviews.


### Evaluation criteria 
The maximum score for the task is **100 points**

1. **Content** — 50 points
   - The presentation includes all the key points mentioned in the "What to tell about" section and is well-structured.
2. **English Proficiency** — 30 points
   - The speaker demonstrates a good command of English, with clear pronunciation and appropriate vocabulary.
3. **Presentation Skills** — 20 points 
   - The speaker maintains good eye contact, uses appropriate body language, and engages the audience effectively.
